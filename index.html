About Me

I am a fourth-year Ph.D. student (enrolled in 2022) in Cyberspace Security at Southeast University, advised by Professor Guilin Qi. My research interests broadly cover multimodal information extraction and knowledge editing & machine unlearning in multimodal large language models (MLLMs).

Currently, my work focuses on machine unlearning in MLLMs, particularly erasing visual concepts to mitigate privacy leakage in multimodal systems. Moving forward, I aim to develop more efficient unlearning methods for MLLMs or text-to-image models to address privacy and safety challenges in multimodal generative AI.
